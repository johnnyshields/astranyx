================================================================================
                         10 NEW GAME IDEAS
================================================================================

Based on the "fun" latent space analysis, here are 10 game concepts with
clearer core loops and more intuitive mechanics.

================================================================================
IDEA 1: GRAVITY FLIP
================================================================================
Genre: Platformer-Puzzle Hybrid
One-Sentence: You can only flip gravity, not move left/right.

MECHANICS:
- Character falls in current gravity direction
- Press SPACE to flip gravity (ceiling becomes floor)
- Collect all gems to open the exit
- Avoid spikes and enemies that patrol platforms
- Later levels: timed switches, crumbling platforms, gravity zones

WHY IT'S FUN:
- One-button simplicity with deep level design potential
- Clear goal (gems -> exit)
- Each flip is consequential (reversibility tension)
- Puzzle-solving meets timing/reflex

VISUAL: Side-view pixel art, character rotates when gravity flips

================================================================================
IDEA 2: WORD SNAKE
================================================================================
Genre: Vocabulary + Snake Mashup
One-Sentence: Classic Snake, but you must spell words to eat letters.

MECHANICS:
- Snake moves continuously (arrow keys to turn)
- Letters spawn on the grid instead of food
- Eat letters in order to spell valid words (3+ letters)
- Completing a word: snake grows, score multiplied by word length
- Eating wrong letter or hitting yourself = game over
- Bonus: longer words = bigger multipliers

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Familiar Snake mechanics with added mental challenge
- Emergent strategy: do I go for "CAT" now or wait for "CATCH"?
- Time pressure (snake keeps moving) vs planning (word building)
- Vocabulary skill rewards experts, short words keep it accessible

VISUAL: Clean minimal grid, letters glow when they'd form valid words

================================================================================
IDEA 3: ECHO MAZE
================================================================================
Genre: Memory/Audio Puzzle
One-Sentence: Navigate a dark maze using only sound echoes.

MECHANICS:
- Screen is mostly black - you see only your character
- Press SPACE to send out a "ping" (sonar pulse)
- Walls briefly illuminate based on distance (closer = brighter)
- Reach the exit before your pings run out
- Enemies appear as red blips on ping
- Memorize the layout between pings

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Novel core mechanic (information scarcity)
- Memory challenge that builds tension
- Resource management (limited pings)
- Satisfying "aha" when mental map clicks

VISUAL: Dark screen, neon pulse waves, walls fade from bright to dark

================================================================================
IDEA 4: STACK ATTACK
================================================================================
Genre: Speed Stacking Action
One-Sentence: Tetris meets Breakout - stack blocks, then launch them.

MECHANICS:
- Blocks fall from top (Tetris-style)
- Complete a row and it becomes a "loaded" projectile
- Press SPACE to launch all loaded rows upward as bullets
- Destroy descending enemies before they reach your stack
- Enemies drop power-ups when destroyed
- If enemies land on your stack, game over

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Two satisfying loops (building AND destroying)
- Strategic tension: build higher for more ammo vs risk of enemies landing
- Cascade potential: perfect row = powerful shot
- Combines two beloved game archetypes

VISUAL: Split focus - building zone below, combat zone above

================================================================================
IDEA 5: COLOR THIEF
================================================================================
Genre: Stealth Puzzle
One-Sentence: Steal colors from objects to become invisible against them.

MECHANICS:
- Top-down stealth game
- You start as white (visible against everything)
- Touch objects to "steal" their color (you become that color)
- Guards can't see you against matching backgrounds
- Reach the vault, steal the treasure, escape
- Obstacles: multi-colored rooms, moving guards, color-locked doors

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Intuitive mechanic (camouflage)
- Clear visual feedback (am I hidden?)
- Puzzle-solving: route planning based on available colors
- Tension from stealth without complex controls

VISUAL: Bold, flat colors. Player character clearly shows current color.

================================================================================
IDEA 6: ORBIT
================================================================================
Genre: Physics Puzzler
One-Sentence: Launch satellites into stable orbits around planets.

MECHANICS:
- Click and drag to set launch angle and power
- Release to launch your satellite
- Satellite affected by gravity of all planets
- Goal: achieve stable orbit (survives 3 full rotations)
- Bonus: collect stars in orbit path
- Later levels: multiple planets, asteroid belts, black holes

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Satisfying physics (watching orbits stabilize)
- Trial and error with clear feedback
- "Almost had it" near-misses encourage retry
- Relaxing pace with challenge depth

VISUAL: Space aesthetic, trajectory preview line, orbital path traces

================================================================================
IDEA 7: DICE DUNGEON
================================================================================
Genre: Roguelike with Dice Combat
One-Sentence: Your abilities are dice - roll them to fight, but they deplete.

MECHANICS:
- Move through dungeon rooms (arrow keys)
- Combat: your "dice pool" shows available dice (d4, d6, d8, etc.)
- Click a die to roll it - deal that much damage
- BUT: each die can only be rolled X times before breaking
- Find new dice as loot
- Enemies have visible HP
- Reach floor 10 to win

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Resource management with randomness
- Risk/reward: save big dice for bosses?
- Roguelike replayability
- Simple combat with strategic depth
- Dice rolling is inherently satisfying

VISUAL: Top-down dungeon, large dice graphics, satisfying roll animations

================================================================================
IDEA 8: PIPELINE PANIC
================================================================================
Genre: Real-time Puzzle (Pipe Dream style)
One-Sentence: Connect pipes before the water reaches the end - but pipes keep breaking.

MECHANICS:
- Water flows from source after 5-second countdown
- Place pipe pieces (randomly given) to guide water to drain
- Completed path = level clear, earn points for extra length
- TWIST: placed pipes randomly crack and leak over time
- Must repair or reroute while water flows
- Multiplayer variant: sabotage opponent's pipes

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Classic pipe mechanic with added chaos
- Planning vs adaptation (pipes break!)
- Time pressure creates panic moments
- Clear success/failure (water reaches drain or doesn't)

VISUAL: Industrial/steampunk, visible water flow, crack effects on pipes

================================================================================
IDEA 9: BOUNCE BACK
================================================================================
Genre: One-Button Arcade
One-Sentence: Ball bounces automatically - you control ONLY the angle of deflection.

MECHANICS:
- Ball bounces around the screen continuously
- Click anywhere to place a temporary deflector paddle at that spot
- Paddle appears for 0.5 seconds then vanishes
- Guide ball to hit all targets
- Avoid hazards (spikes, enemies)
- Ball speeds up over time

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Incredibly simple (just click)
- Requires prediction and timing
- Satisfying geometry/angles
- Easy to learn, hard to master
- "One more try" quick restarts

VISUAL: Minimalist, clean geometry, particle trails on ball

================================================================================
IDEA 10: MERGE KINGDOM
================================================================================
Genre: Merge Puzzle + Tower Defense
One-Sentence: Merge soldiers to create stronger units, then deploy them against waves.

MECHANICS:
- Grid on left: merge zone. Drag matching units together to upgrade.
- Path on right: enemies march toward your castle
- Drag units from merge zone to defense slots along path
- Units auto-attack enemies in range
- Earn gold from kills to buy more basic units
- Survive 20 waves to win

WHY IT'S FUN:
- Two satisfying loops (merging + defending)
- Clear progression (units visibly upgrade)
- Strategic decisions: merge more or deploy now?
- Wave structure gives breathing room + urgency cycles

VISUAL: Medieval/fantasy, clear unit tiers, satisfying merge animations
